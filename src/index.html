What does "async" mean?

It's short for asynchronous. And in this context it refers to "how" we communicate within the team.

In contrast, synchronous communication usually refers to an immediate "face-to-face" exchange of information. Like having a meeting with people that are all present in the same room.

Asynchronous communication usually refers to an exchange of information that happens over time. Like sending and receiving emails. Here, a response doesn't have to (nor is expected to!) be immediate.

What's a standup?

It's a common ritual among agile/scrum teams. Usually every day all team members will gather in person, and everyone will answer 3 questions:

  1. What did you work on yesterday?
  2. What will you work on today?
  3. Is there anything that prevents you from doing your work?

Why are standups useful?

Because they make the team aware of what everyone is working on, and what they might be struggling with.

In general, standups improve the flow of information. They help participants with getting a deeper understanding of the problem space they are working in. They stimulate everyone to help each other, and share/synchronize their understanding of shared goals.

What's the problem then?

The problem is that a fixed time must be picked for the entire team to be available. And the actual time the standup takes place often disrupts people's workflow—due to context switching, "deep work" interference and scheduling conflicts.
These problems also tend to be more prominent in a remote working environment, where people have different "work rhythms", because of personal preference and/or timezone differences.

Other issues with the traditional synchronous standup are:

  • Discussions during the standup are common (sidebar!).
  • Participants can "zone out", get distracted or loose interest.
  • There's no efficient way to "replay" the standup.
  • There's usually no record of previous standups.
